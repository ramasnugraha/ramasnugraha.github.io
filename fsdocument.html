Introduction

At TRAC, we always do our best to optimize user satisfaction.

However, all this time, our focus is always on the business (B2B) users, and we are rarely aware of our personal/individual (B2C) users. Since in recent years, our personal users are increasing, that makes us think that we need to expand our reach to the personal market. The research team also found that the market potential is significantly high.

However, this step raises a new challenge. Currently, the best way to reach personal users is through a mobile app, and our mobile app is still lagging behind in innovation and having low user experience satisfaction. Therefore, despite recognizing the immense potential within the personal users market, we've struggled to capture their attention and engagement.

To address this, we've decided to take a bold step: We will merge business and personal products and users onto a single superapp platform.

Purpose of Document

This Functional Specification Document (FSD) serves as the foundational guide for the comprehensive development of the innovative TRACtoGo mobile application. Its primary purpose is to translate the high-level business needs outlined in the Business Requirement Document (BRD) into detailed functional specifications, ensuring that the final product successfully delivers all the suggested requirements and business expectations. The FSD is a critical bridge between business objectives and technical implementation, offering a holistic view of the application's intended functionality and user experience.

This FSD focuses specifically on the Homepage and Car Rent features of the new TRACtoGo. Other features are detailed in the other FSD, and you can check it in the FSD repository. For these two features, Homepage and Car Rent, this document outlines essential components to guide the development process.

The FSD aims to minimize ambiguities, facilitate effective communication among stakeholders (developers, testers, business analysts, and product owners), and ensure that the TRACtoGo mobile application is developed efficiently, accurately, and in full alignment with the business requirements.

Scope of Project

The rebuild of TRACtoGo will take time and we're going step by step considering the amount of resources that we have. Therefore, we need to separate the iteration into multiple parts that are possible to accomplish. In this iteration, the scope of the development will be limited to:

  1. Merging the business and personal accounts.

  2. Switching accounts from business and personal.

  3. A new flow of car rent from start to end (from booking process to payment).

System Overview

TRACtoGo app is now entering a new phase that will serve both business and personal users together in one ecosystem. The app is expected to be more user-friendly, intuitive, and have better flows and user experiences. The new app will also provide better account management, and the service is not limited to the rent itself, but also for requesting maintenance for the rented vehicles.

Objective

The first phase of release will introduce three major features aimed at simplifying account access, enhancing usability, and streamlining the car rental journey. These consist of:

  1. For both business and personal users, they should be able to log in and use the one TRACtoGo app. Users can access one app to manage personal or corporate rentals, selecting either ‘Login as Business’ or ‘Login as Personal’ directly from the login screen. This integration allows seamless switching between business and individual contexts, reducing confusion and improving overall efficiency.

  2. Both business and personal users are able to switch accounts. The homepage now offers an intuitive account switching feature. Users can quickly transition between business and personal profiles without logging out, making it easy for those who manage rentals in both contexts to access all relevant data and actions in one place.

  3. The booking flow will be simplified. The new flow connects vehicle booking directly to payment completion, ensuring users have a clear, guided path from rental selection through to successful transaction. This update reduces friction, clarifies progress, and provides timely feedback at each step of the journey.

Account Login

The new version of TRACtoGo must cater for both business (B2B) and personal (B2C) needs.

Use Cases

This use case shows how the actors and the system interact with each other.

User Flow

The new login flow is different from the previous one. This time, the user will need to choose their type of account at the beginning before they input the username. Nevertheless, the flow should be simple and easy to follow.

Specifications:

  1. The business account is created outside the application and given directly offline by our operation team. Also, it is only created for individuals whose companies already have a business partnership with TRAC.

  2. Personal users cannot create a business account through the application.

  3. For personal users, if they haven't created the account, they must create an account first before they are able to log in. The register flow is provided separately from this document.

  4. Data the users need to input when they're going to login:

    1. Email address (business for business account and personal for personal account)

System Flow

The system is created to help the technical team understand clearly for how the merging business and personal login accounts works.

Specifications:

  1. For business accounts, currently the login requirements are only the company email. It doesn't accept the mobile phone number like the personal account.

  2. For business accounts, the email must contain the domain name of the company's name. Other domains (example: gmail.com or others) won't be accepted.

Switch Account

User might have both business and personal accounts. Therefore, the user should be able to switch between their accounts practically. The switching account feature expected to give more benefit to users for the ease with which they're going to use their account to do the transaction.

Use Cases

This use case shows how the actors and the system interact with each other.

User Flow

The entry point to switch accounts should be accessible and easy to find. Therefore, we decided to put the entry point at the homepage. They can always see it anytime they open the application and arrive at the homepage.

Specifications:

  1. The flow indicated that the user already has and logged in for their business and personal accounts.

  2. Personal accounts don’t always have business accounts. But business accounts can always—if they want—have the personal account.

System Flow

What is going on in the background for this feature should be simple. If the user hasn’t logged in to a personal account, then they're still going to need to log in first.

Specifications:

  1. When the user doesn't log in, the switching account feature needs to direct the user to the login page. From there, they need to choose either logging in using a business or personal account.

  2. The UX Team should consider a way out for: What if the user doesn't have a business account, and they cannot create a business account by themselves? We need to give a proper explanation to the user.

  3. If the business user turns out to have not created a personal account, they need to create the account first. The register flow is explained in a separate document.

Rent a Car

The new car rent flow must be more simple than the old version of TRACtoGo.

Although we're planning to deliver a better version, some constraints remain persistent and will be explained briefly in the Assumptions and Constraints section.

Use Cases

This use case shows how the actors and the system interact with each other.

User Flow

For both business and personal account should have same flow. Most of details is the same, especially for the page list which the business account can see much more wider range of cars.

Specifications:

  1. Personal users only can see the car list for personal users. For business users, if they have a personal user account, then they can see the car list for the personal account.

  2. Data the user needs to input when they're going to rent a car:

    1. Rent date

    2. Rent location

    3. Rent package (Hour/Daily/Monthly/Yearly)

    4. With driver or self-drive

System Flow

The rent a car flow is crucial. All stakeholders need to make sure the flow is flawless considering that now there will be two types of accounts in one product.

Specifications:

  1. The price is hidden for business users that have personal users. Further discussion is needed for this price appearance. The business team still needs time to make decisions.

  2. Checking car availability that a user has already booked currently takes under 5 minutes, but should take more time than expected. If the car turns out to be unavailable, then we need to redirect the user back to the page list.

  3. The UX Team should prepare the notification for which the car is not available to rent.

  4. The car list that appears in the business account is different from the car list that appears in the personal account. For the business account, the vehicle list in the business account has a wider range.

Non-Functional Requirements

This section tells any requirements that are outside the functional features. For now, at least we have some concerns to discuss further about the technical things such as:

  • The system must load any main page within 2 seconds under normal network conditions.

  • The app must reliably support up to 20,000 concurrent users without service degradation during peak periods.

  • All communication must be encrypted (TLS 1.2 or above); user credentials must be stored securely and comply with national data protection laws.

  • The service must be available 99.9% of the time, excluding scheduled maintenance. (This requirement still needs further discussion with the Operation team in the field.)

  • The app must automatically recover from server crashes and ensure no bookings or data are lost.

  • The system should allow a new user to complete a rental with no more than five taps/clicks and provide clear error messages for failures.

  • All code must be fully documented, and automated deployment should be supported to allow bug fixes and minor updates without taking the system down.

  • The application must function on iOS 15+ and Android 10+.

  • Payment systems must comply with PCI DSS as the security standards. The application must not collect or store sensitive information unless necessary for business/legal reasons.

Assumptions and Constraints

Some constraints remain while we're trying to develop a better TRACtoGo application. For the three new features here, there are still assumptions and constraints that need to be solved in the near future and next iteration of the product.

The car availability data is not real-time. Our servers sometimes cannot get the real data update from the field regarding which car was already rented on that day. This means there will be a chance when a user books a car that they see available in the page list, only to find out it's not. And they will need to look for another car because we will force them to do that by redirecting them back to the page list.

Acceptance Criteria

This section defines the conditions that must be met for the features to be considered complete and delivered.

Account Login

  • User can log in for both business and personal use on the same page.

  • The system can detect and reject the request if the user is trying to enter the business account using a personal account.

  • The system acknowledges all the domains of the companies that have been in partnership with TRAC and are currently using TRAC services.

  • Users can log in smoothly when their account and OTP are correct.

Switch Account

  • User can access the “Switch Account” feature from the homepage after login.

  • User with both business and personal accounts can successfully switch and see the alternate dashboard within 5 seconds.

  • Switching account updates all displayed and accessible data to the selected context; no cross-account leakage.

  • If a user tries to switch but only has one account type logged in, the system direct user to login page.

Rent a Car

The following point must be accomplished to consider the features successfully developed:

  • User can select a car, input booking details, proceed to payment, and get a confirmation message after successful booking.

  • All required booking fields must be validated before payment is enabled.

  • A failed payment attempt triggers a clear error and allows retrying or exiting the flow.

  • After completion, the user receives a summary of the booking by email and in-app notification.
